How it works

Built around the real rhythm of a household.

01

Brain dump the mess.

Type a loose list of household thoughts. FairNest suggests structured cards, then makes you review every suggestion before anything is saved.

02

Organize the load.

Filter by today, this week, recurring responsibilities, decisions, appreciations, or everything. Swipe to finish, snooze, remove, or undo.

03

Check in weekly.

Capture what felt heavy, what got done, what needs ownership, and one appreciation. Only reviewed changes update the board.

Native iPhone experience

Feels like it belongs on the phone you already use.

FairNest uses familiar iOS patterns: large titles, tab navigation, menus, swipe actions, widgets, local notifications, Dynamic Type, and clear privacy controls.

Features

A complete system for shared home responsibility.

Load cards

Track tasks, recurring responsibilities, decisions, reminders, conversations, and appreciation with ownership and effort.

Brain Dump

Turn loose thoughts into reviewable starter cards using on-device intelligence when available, with a private local parser fallback.

Recurring rhythm

Daily, weekly, and monthly responsibilities roll forward without turning the whole home into a noisy project board.

Appreciation built in

Household labor is not only chores. FairNest gives gratitude and recognition a place beside the work.

Gentle reminders

Local notifications can remind you about due cards, recurring responsibilities, and weekly check-ins after permission is granted.

Private pairing

Use solo mode, or invite a partner through private CloudKit Sharing. There are no FairNest accounts or passwords.

Widgets

See the next responsibility, today's load, or a weekly overview from the Home Screen or Lock Screen.

Privacy controls

Export local data, delete local data, manage reminders, and keep iCloud Sync off unless you choose to turn it on.

Private by design

The household stays in the household.

FairNest is local-first. iCloud Sync is optional. Partner sharing uses private CloudKit Sharing. There is no custom backend, no third-party analytics, no ads, no subscriptions, and no data selling.
